## Primary source (the pre-mortem)

- **Klein, G. — "Performing a Project Premortem."** *Harvard Business Review*
  (September 2007). <https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem>
  Used in Gate 3: the canonical pre-mortem method. Assuming the project has
  *already failed* (prospective hindsight) elicits more, and more concrete,
  causes than asking what might go wrong — Klein cites Mitchell, Russo &
  Pennington's finding that prospective hindsight raises the ability to identify
  reasons for a future outcome by ~30%. Also legitimizes dissent: the doubter is
  performing an exercise, not attacking the team.

## Concepts and their public references (verified to load)

- **Steelmanning** — Gate 1: repair the argument to its strongest form before
  attacking it. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steelmanning>

- **Survivorship bias** — Gate 2 alternative: you see only the cases that
  survived to be observed. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Survivorship_bias>

- **Texas sharpshooter fallacy** — Gate 2 alternative: a pattern selected after
  searching many candidates (target drawn around the cluster).
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Texas_sharpshooter_fallacy>

- **Multiple comparisons problem** — Gate 2: searching over many features /
  thresholds inflates the chance the best-looking one is noise.
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiple_comparisons_problem>

- **Groupthink** — Gate 3: independent idea generation before discussion defeats
  conformity pressure. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Groupthink>

- **Sunk cost** — Gate 4: continuing because of past investment rather than
  future expected value. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunk_cost>

- **Argument from authority** — Gate 4: a claim accepted for *who* said it, not
  the evidence; re-evaluate with the name removed.
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argument_from_authority>

- **Reference class forecasting** — Gate 5: anchor a forecast on the outside view
  / base rate of the class before the inside-view specifics.
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reference_class_forecasting>

- **Base rate fallacy** — Gate 5: neglecting the prior probability of the class
  when judging a specific case. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Base_rate_fallacy>

- **Falsifiability** — Gate 6: a claim that excludes no observable outcome is not
  testable. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falsifiability>

- **Hindsight bias** — Gate 6: a kill-criteria contract written *after* the
  outcome is worthless because memory of what you "expected" is rewritten.
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindsight_bias>

## Incentive audit (Gate 4)

- **Farnam Street — "Bias from Incentives and Reinforcement."** Incentives shape
  conclusions below the level of conscious dishonesty.
  <https://fs.blog/bias-incentives-reinforcement/>

- **Farnam Street — "Charlie Munger's System of Mental Models / Operating
  System."** Munger's checklist of the psychology of human misjudgment; "never
  ask a barber if you need a haircut." <https://fs.blog/munger-operating-system/>

## Calibration and the outside view (Gate 5)

- **Tetlock, P. & Gardner, D. — *Superforecasting: The Art and Science of
  Prediction*** (Crown, 2015). Calibration is a trainable skill; the outside
  view and well-calibrated intervals beat confident inside-view narratives.
  Overview: <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superforecasting>

- **Kahneman, D. — *Thinking, Fast and Slow*** (FSG, 2011). The inside vs outside
  view, base-rate neglect, and overconfidence of plan-based forecasts (the
  "planning fallacy").

- **Galef, J. — *The Scout Mindset*** (Portfolio, 2021). Seeking truth over
  defending a position; "what evidence would change my mind?" as the operative
  test of a held belief (Gate 6).
